WAKE DA SHINKAFA

DESCRIPTION

Tuwon Masara is a traditional Nigerian dish made from maize (corn) flour. It is a staple food in Northern Nigeria and is commonly served with soups such as Miyan Kuka, Miyan Taushe, or Miyan Kubewa.

preparation involves cooking maize flour in boiling water to form a thick, smooth, and stretchy swallow. It has a slightly coarse texture compared to other types of tuwo, such as Tuwon Shinkafa (rice swallow). The taste is mildly sweet and earthy, making it a perfect complement to rich and flavorful soups. Tuwon Masara is best enjoyed hot, usually molded into small portions and dipped into soup. It is a filling and energy- rich meal, making it a favorite among many households.
